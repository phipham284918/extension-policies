Privacy Policy for Tone Ripple

Last updated: 6/2/2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Tone Ripple handles information when you use this Firefox add-on. Based on the reviewed technical inputs, the extension appears focused on local browser functionality and local settings storage.

1. Overview

We are committed to respecting user privacy. This Add-on is designed to operate with minimal data use and to collect or transmit only the information necessary for its functionality.

Our goal is to keep the Add-on focused on its stated purpose while limiting data use to what is reasonably required for core features, compatibility, and user-selected behavior.

2. What Information the Add-on Uses

By default, the Add-on does not collect personal information.

The Add-on may store preferences and settings locally on your device using browser storage features so that it can remember your configuration and work properly.

When the Add-on handles information, it should do so in a way that remains closely tied to the feature the user is invoking, the settings the user has chosen, and the temporary browser state needed to make the Add-on work correctly. Any broader data practice should be clearly disclosed before publication.

3. Local Storage

The Add-on may store the following information locally on your device:

  • User settings and preferences
  • Saved configuration related to the Add-on’s features
  • Temporary local data needed to provide the Add-on’s functionality

This data remains on your device unless you remove it or uninstall the Add-on.

Local storage may help the Add-on preserve settings across sessions, restore expected functionality when the browser restarts, and reduce the need for users to repeatedly reconfigure the same options.

4. Data Transmission

The Add-on does not transmit personal information to us by default.

If a future version of the Add-on introduces optional online features or remote services, this Privacy Policy will be updated to explain what data is transmitted, for what purpose, and under what user controls or consent.

If remote communication exists in a particular build, the final published policy should explain whether it is necessary for the feature, whether user content or identifiers are transmitted, how long related data is retained, and whether users can avoid that transmission by not using the optional feature.

5. Permissions and Browser Access

The Add-on only requests permissions that are relevant to its intended functionality. Such permissions may include access to browser storage or the active tab, depending on the Add-on’s features. These permissions are not used for unrelated tracking, profiling, or advertising unless explicitly disclosed here.

Browser permissions are intended to support the Add-on’s visible features and user-initiated actions. They should not be understood as permission to collect unrelated personal information or to monitor browsing activity beyond what the Add-on’s documented behavior requires.

6. Third Parties

We do not sell personal data, and we do not share personal information with third parties for advertising, analytics, or marketing purposes unless explicitly disclosed here.

If third-party hosting, synchronization, analytics, support tooling, or similar services are introduced later, this policy should be revised to identify those services and explain any resulting data handling with appropriate specificity.

7. User Control

You can stop local data use by uninstalling the Add-on and clearing any related browser storage. If the Add-on later includes optional data transmission features, appropriate user controls and disclosures should be provided.

Where practical, users should also be able to review and change saved preferences through the Add-on itself or through standard browser controls. Removing local browser storage may reset saved options or related functional state.

8. Security

We make reasonable efforts to limit data usage and to keep the Add-on focused on its stated functionality.

This includes trying to avoid unnecessary retention, limiting access to stored information, and revising implementation choices over time when needed to reduce privacy risk while preserving the Add-on’s core features.

9. Children’s Privacy

The Add-on is not intended for children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected by updating the “Last updated” date above.

Updated versions of this policy should continue to match the Add-on’s real behavior, especially if permissions, local storage patterns, remote features, or third-party services change over time.
